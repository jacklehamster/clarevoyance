# CLAREVOYANCE — Full Story & Character Reference

---

## The World

A fantasy world where powerful individuals can manipulate magic and the elements.
Witches, sorcerers, and other gifted beings exist alongside ordinary people. Magic
is rare and personal — each individual's power is distinct to them.

---

## Protagonist: Clare

Clare is a young woman who has possessed the gift of clairvoyance since birth — the
ability to see the future. She experiences both long-range visions and immediate
foresight: a split second ahead of the present, enough to see translucent shims of
things about to happen. An outline of a rock before it falls. The ghost of a fist
before it lands.

She has never been wrong.

**This is the game's central mechanic.** Players see translucent artifacts of incoming
attacks moments before they occur. Over time, Clare's foresight upgrades — she sees
further ahead, with more clarity. This progression is the RPG system. Every upgrade
changes how the game *feels*.

---

## The Inciting Vision

Clare wakes from a dream in which she sees her King assassinated. She cannot identify
the killer, but she recognizes the setting — the decorations, the banners, the
cobblestones of the winter solstice festival. She knows which festival. She knows how
much time she has.

She sets out immediately. She has never been wrong. The King must be saved.

What she does not yet understand is that she has already misread everything.

---

## Central Theme

**Things are not what they appear to be.**

Every boss in Clarevoyance teaches this lesson in a different form. Hiri's power was
invisible helpers. The Cat Wizard was the cat. The giants were tiny girls. Amblico's
attacks were lies. The modern world was an illusion. The King's death was always
Clare's doing.

Clare can see the future. She has never been wrong. And yet she misreads everything —
until the moment she finally sees clearly, and what she sees most clearly is herself.

---

## Bosses

### Hiri — The Hands of God
An undefeated champion with a legendary reputation for telekinesis — striking opponents
from impossible distances with pure mental force. Clare faces him with confidence. Her
foresight will show her the attacks.

It does. But the shims keep appearing from places Hiri isn't standing — from empty air
to her left, her right, behind her. She is getting beaten despite seeing every attack
coming.

Then she understands. She stops fighting Hiri entirely and begins striking at nothing.
Two invisible beings fall one by one. Without them, Hiri is ordinary — theatrical,
perhaps skilled, but ordinary. The undefeated champion was a fraud, protected by
invisible helpers he took full credit for.

*Mechanic: shims appear from empty space. The real threat is invisible.*

---

### Mochi — The Cat Wizard
Clare encounters an unremarkable man wandering the road, cradling a small cat. The cat
watches her with ancient, knowing eyes.

The battle is one of the hardest of her life. Fireballs, precise enchantments, the
terrain conspiring against her. The man barely moves. Gradually, unmistakably, Clare
realizes the cat is directing everything.

She defeats Mochi.

Standing over the small creature, she feels the victory go hollow. Mochi was never
evil. He was simply powerful, and powerful things attract conflict. The man blinks at
her, apparently unaware anything significant happened.

Mochi was on the good side all along. This encounter was a misunderstanding — Clare
read the danger correctly but the intent wrongly.

Clare carries Mochi to his family — a tender, unplanned detour outside her mission.
He returns to them with quiet dignity. She watches, then continues on her way.

*Theme: some things are not about the mission. Clare read the threat correctly but
misread the meaning entirely.*

*Mechanic: the obvious target is wrong — the cat is the real threat.*

---

### Spirolo
Spirolo is impossibly fast — a blur, untouchable, striking with lethal accuracy. Even
with her foresight, Clare barely has time to react. She is losing badly.

She makes a desperate gamble: she calls on Desiris Winter, the ice witch who answers
to no one and hates both sides equally. Desiris comes out of curiosity and contempt.

Together they drive Spirolo into water. His speed is significantly reduced by water —
still dangerous, but manageable. Desiris freezes the water at the precise moment.
Spirolo stops.

In the silence afterward, Clare and Desiris stand on opposite sides of the ice. Two
warriors with no alliance. No trust. No reason to be anything but enemies.

They turn on each other.

*Mechanic: speed exceeds Clare's reaction time — cooperation and environment required.*
*Secret: water is Spirolo's weakness. He is significantly slowed by it.*

---

### Clare vs. Desiris / Dr. Mad
The fight between Clare and Desiris is brutal and honest — two warriors at full
strength, neither yielding. They are evenly matched in ways neither expected. The
battle drags on until both are exhausted.

Neither sees Dr. Mad coming.

He belongs to no faction, no court, no prophecy. A scientist of extraordinary and
terrible intelligence who operates entirely outside the fantasy world's logic —
anachronistic, mysterious, connected to no one. He collects what interests him.
He takes them both.

---

### The Modern World

Clare wakes with machinery humming around her. Wires trail from a headpiece she tears
away. Her clothing is thin, unfamiliar, skimpy — nothing like her own. Her power is
gone. The environment is unsettling, made of materials she has no words for. Light
comes from sources with no flame.

She finds Desiris on the floor nearby, screaming — powerless, unable to walk or stand,
writhing in agony. Whatever Dr. Mad did to her in the experiments has taken something
fundamental from her physically.

Clare, who has every reason to leave her, does not. She finds a chair with wheels.
She helps Desiris into it.

They navigate the world together — it is our modern world, though neither character
has any knowledge of or memory of it. They have no map, no rules, no context for
anything around them. Clare pushes the wheelchair through streets that make no sense
to her. Desiris takes it all in with the openness of someone who has nothing left
to protect.

Days pass. Something unexpected happens to Desiris. She has lost everything — her
power, her kingdom, her army, her identity — and finds a kind of peace. No enemies.
No throne to maintain. Just cold clean air, strange food, the sound of Clare's
footsteps behind her, and small surprising kindnesses passing between them.

She becomes almost happy.

Clare does not. She is relentless, obsessive, burning with the certainty that none
of this is real. She cannot accept the present. She cannot stop moving toward a
future she can no longer see clearly.

Desiris watches her with something like sadness. She has learned to let go of
everything. Clare cannot let go of anything.

A bond forms between them quietly, without announcement — borderline romantic, never
named, real in the way that things forged under impossible circumstances are real.

Clare is right: the modern world is entirely an illusion — Dr. Mad's masterwork, a
constructed reality so complete that most minds would simply inhabit it and forget.
Clare's clairvoyance, stubborn and relentless, kept pulling at the edges of the
illusion until they frayed. She finds the way back.

They return to the fantasy world together and defeat Dr. Mad with the efficiency of
two people who have learned each other's rhythms.

Then it is over. The kingdoms are still at war. Nothing has changed except them,
and that changes nothing. They part without a fight — no dramatic farewell, no
promises. Desiris turns back toward her world. Clare watches her go, then turns
back toward her mission.

Desiris is never the same afterward. She returns to her cold kingdom, powerful
and respected and alone, and those who know her notice a quietness — as if she
is always half-listening for something that isn't there. She never speaks of it.

---

### The Twin Dolls
Clare finds a long narrow bridge crossing darkness — floor below invisible, ceiling
above the same. No wind. No sound. Just the bridge, stretching further than it should.

She is halfway across before she feels them. Two vast presences, one on each side.
Enormous figures in deep crimson and black kimonos, gold trim immaculate, porcelain
white faces with painted red lips pulled into fixed grins. Their heads tilt in
unison. One points. The other is already laughing — a vibration felt in the bridge,
in her sternum, in the back of her teeth.

She runs. Her foresight shows shims of enormous fingers descending and she throws
herself sideways, rolls, keeps moving. They pace her easily, reaching down with
lazy amusement. They are playing with her. They are delighted.

She reaches the end of the bridge and spins around —

Two girls look up at her. Remarkably short. The same porcelain faces, the same
painted lips, the same crimson kimonos, the same kanzashi hairpins. They could not
reach her shoulder.

One points. The other covers her mouth, shoulders shaking with laughter — high,
bright, completely unhinged.

The fight is the most technically demanding of her journey. Not because either
twin is stronger, but because they are never separate. Every attack is mirrored
perfectly — dodge one and you step into the other. The only path through is the
sliver of future her gift can barely find if she looks hard enough.

She finds it. Again and again. Smaller each time.

When it is over the twins sit on the ground leaning against each other, still
smiling their fixed painted smiles — satisfied, as if the whole thing was exactly
as entertaining as they'd hoped. As Clare walks away, she hears one of them giggle.

*Mechanic: attacks are perfectly mirrored — dodging one leads into the other.
The solution is an increasingly narrow window of safe movement.*

---

### Amblico — The Mindbender
Amblico was Clare's closest friend in childhood.

His power does not attack her body. It attacks her gift — seeping into her
clairvoyance like ink into water, showing her attacks that aren't coming, hiding
the ones that are. Fighting him is like fighting blindfolded in a room full of mirrors.
Every shim is a potential lie.

She survives by instinct and stubbornness, at great cost.

*Secret: Amblico turned evil under the influence of his younger sister, Skivana —
who had always resented Clare and the closeness she shared with her brother.*

*Mechanic: shims are unreliable — some are false positives, some attacks have no shim.*

---

### Skivana
Skivana has hated Clare since childhood with the pure uncomplicated hatred of someone
who watched something precious be taken without understanding it was never hers.
She influenced her brother's fall deliberately.

Her power is different from Amblico's — she cannot corrupt Clare's clairvoyance
directly. Instead she bends all other perception: gravity inverts, sounds become
images, touch becomes color, the ground becomes sky and then becomes nothing.

Clare cannot fight what she cannot trust her senses to identify.

She overcomes it by going inward — past the hallucinations, past the fear, to the
quiet place beneath all perception where her gift lives. She finds the shims even
in the chaos. She follows them home.

Skivana falls, furious and disbelieving.

*Mechanic: the environment itself becomes unreliable. Only the shims can be trusted —
and even finding them requires focus under sensory chaos.*

---

### Final Boss: King Dev
The King's castle is exactly as Clare saw it in her dream. The winter solstice banners.
The cobblestones. The torches.

King Dev has no magical power whatsoever. What he has is rarer and more dangerous —
a complete understanding of how Clare thinks. He has studied her, perhaps for years.
He fakes attacks with perfect precision, drawing her shims toward ghosts. He speaks
to her quietly and deliberately during combat, finding the places where doubt lives
and pressing on them.

*"You've been wrong before. Haven't you."*

She has. Every misread rises in her mind at exactly the moment he intends.

She fights through it. She always fights through it.

And then the final vision comes — the true one, the one she has been carrying since
the beginning. The King falling. The winter solstice banners. The cobblestones
beneath her feet right now.

She sees the hand that strikes him.

She recognizes it. It is her own.

*Mechanic: shims are faked by a non-magical opponent who has simply studied Clare
deeply. The real challenge is psychological, not supernatural.*

---

## The Twist

The prophecy was never wrong. It was never misread. She simply could not imagine,
when she first saw it, that the future she was racing to prevent was one she would
choose.

Along the way she learned the truth: the King was responsible for her father's death.
His ambitions, unchecked, would fracture the kingdom she crossed the world to protect.
She had all the information from the beginning. She just didn't have the context yet.

Clare was never saving the King. She was always going to be the one who killed him.
Every step of the journey was the path to this moment.

She sheathes her weapon in the aftermath. The future is uncertain. For the first time
in her life, she finds she doesn't mind.

---

## Epilogue

*Somewhere far away, in a cold kingdom that runs itself without her attention, Desiris
sits at a window and watches snow fall. She catches a flake on her fingertip and holds
it — not with power, just with stillness — until it melts.*

*She almost smiles.*

## Desiris Winter — Character Background

Desiris grew up as the daughter of a powerful warlord who built his kingdom on cold
ambition and raised her as his instrument. She became exactly what he intended — cold,
still, devastating — and felt nothing about this for a very long time.

A hero named Aiden arrived fighting against her father's forces. They kept encountering
each other in battle, developing a reluctant mutual respect neither acknowledged.
Desiris intervened in a battle on his behalf without fully deciding to. They formed
an awkward alliance — no name, no announcement — and developed a fighting rhythm
without discussing it.

She privately decided she didn't want him to die. This was new.

Her father's castle was the inevitable destination. She confronted him alone while
Aiden fought toward the center. She won. But the castle began to crumble.

Aiden came back for her when he should have escaped.

She froze the ceiling — every crack sealed, every fracture locked in ice, the collapse
suspended long enough for two people to walk out. She took his arm. They walked out
into the cold night together.

They traveled for a while afterward. No destination. She enjoyed it and would not
have admitted this. They parted quietly and mutually, and she went north to rebuild
her kingdom. She did not look back, though she wanted to.

Years passed. She was respected. She was alone.

News reached her that Aiden had acquired power beyond imagination — power that was
corrupting him. A group of young fighters came to her, desperate, asking for help.
She said yes.

She found him and tried words first — reminded him of who he had been, of every
moment that mattered. She could see it landing and failing to overcome what surrounded it.

She cried for the first time in her life. Her tears froze before they reached her
chin and fell to the ground as small perfect crystals that caught the light and shattered.

She fought the hardest fight of her life. She won.

She left without waiting for him to speak. She went home. Her kingdom was cold and
it prospered and she was alone.

She still sometimes stood at her window watching snow fall, thinking about things
she had held with perfect discipline, and all the things that had fallen anyway.

*Note: this backstory is a parallel narrative involving a separate protagonist (Aiden)
and is not part of Clarevoyance's main plot. However, it defines Desiris' emotional
history and explains the particular quietness Clare leaves behind in her — Desiris has
already lost one person she did not name what she felt for. She will not speak of
Clare either.*

## Dr. Mad — Character Notes

Dr. Mad is a one-off antagonist with no name beyond his title and no connection to
any faction, court, or prophecy in the fantasy world. He is anachronistic —
a scientist who operates entirely outside the world's logic, inspired by the
cross-genre spirit of classic RPGs like Phantasy Star.

He is not evil in a conventional sense. He is simply a man of extraordinary and
terrible intelligence who collects what interests him. Clare and Desiris, mid-battle
and mutually exhausted, interest him.

His masterwork is the illusory modern world — a constructed reality so complete that
most minds would simply inhabit it. It is defeated not by force but by Clare's
stubborn clairvoyance refusing to accept what it cannot verify.

He is defeated after Clare and Desiris return to the fantasy world, efficiently and
without ceremony, by two people who have learned each other's rhythms.

He does not need to be explained. His inexplicability is the point.

## Boss Design Summary

| Boss | What It Tests | Subverts Clairvoyance By |
|------|--------------|--------------------------|
| Hiri | Observation | Shims appear from empty space — real threat is invisible |
| Mochi | Assumptions | The obvious target is wrong — the cat is the threat |
| Spirolo | Adaptability | Speed exceeds reaction time — cooperation and environment required |
| Dr. Mad / Modern World | Acceptance | Clare's power cannot verify the illusion — only persistence breaks it |
| Twin Dolls | Precision | Perfect mirroring — dodging one leads into the other |
| Amblico | Trust | Shims are corrupted — some false, some missing |
| Skivana | Focus | All senses are unreliable — only the gift can be trusted |
| King Dev | Certainty | No magic — just a man who has studied Clare deeply |
